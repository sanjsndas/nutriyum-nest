Wholesome Recipes for Real Life

Sunshine Smoothie Bowl

Start your day with vibrant energy! This delightful smoothie bowl is packed with colorful fruits and superfoods. Customize with your favorite fruits, seeds, and toppings. Simple to prepare, ideal for busy mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe banana, frozen
  • 1 cup frozen mango chunks
  • 1/2 cup frozen pineapple chunks
  • 1/2 cup plant-based milk (almond, oat, or coconut)
  • 1 scoop vanilla plant-based protein powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds or flax seeds
  • Optional: 1/4 avocado or 1 tbsp almond butter for creaminess
Toppings (choose your favorites):
  • Granola
  • Fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Sliced kiwi
  • Shredded coconut
  • Bee pollen
  • Goji berries
  • A drizzle of raw honey or maple syrup
Instructions:
  1. Combine frozen banana, mango, pineapple, plant-based milk, protein powder (if using), chia/flax seeds, and optional avocado/almond butter in a high-speed blender.
  2. Blend on high until smooth and creamy. Add more milk, one tablespoon at a time, if needed.
  3. Pour into a bowl.
  4. Arrange your desired toppings.
  5. Serve immediately.
Nutrition Tips: Rich in fiber, vitamins C and A, and healthy fats. Protein and fiber keep you full and energized.

Cozy Lentil Soup

A warm, comforting, and deeply satisfying lentil soup. Easy to make, uses simple pantry staples, and packed with plant-based protein and fiber. Brown or green lentils provide essential nutrients. Tastes even better the next day and freezes beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1.5 cups brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups fresh spinach or kale, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (optional)
Instructions:
  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Sauté onion, carrots, and celery for 8-10 minutes.
  2. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, and turmeric. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Stir in rinsed lentils, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes.
  5. Remove bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. If using, stir in spinach or kale and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Serve hot.
Nutrition Tips: Excellent source of plant-based protein and fiber, rich in iron and vitamins.

Speedy Salmon with Roasted Asparagus

A nutritious and delicious meal in under 30 minutes! Packed with flavor, lean protein, and essential nutrients. Salmon is rich in Omega-3s, protein, and vitamins. Asparagus is high in fiber and vitamins. Both are seasoned with olive oil, lemon, salt, and pepper, then roasted on one sheet for minimal cleanup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 (6 oz) salmon fillets, skin on or off
  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus, tough ends trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 1 lemon, half sliced for roasting, half for juice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: fresh dill or parsley for garnish
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet.
  2. Toss asparagus with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper on the baking sheet.
  3. Place salmon fillets on the same sheet. Drizzle with remaining 1 tbsp olive oil, season, and top with lemon slices.
  4. Roast for 12-18 minutes until salmon flakes and asparagus is tender-crisp.
  5. Remove from oven. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over top.
  6. Serve immediately.
Nutrition Tips: Rich in Omega-3 fatty acids for heart and brain health, high-quality protein, and fiber from asparagus.

Vibrant Quinoa & Black Bean Salad

A powerhouse of nutrition and flavor, perfect for a light yet satisfying lunch or vibrant side. This plant-based salad combines wholesome quinoa (complete protein, fiber, minerals) with hearty black beans (protein, fiber). Fresh bell peppers, corn, red onion, and cilantro add color and nutrients. The zesty lime vinaigrette ties all flavors together. Easy to prepare and holds up beautifully for meal prepping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 (15-ounce) can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
For the Lime Vinaigrette:
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 tsp maple syrup or agave nectar for a touch of sweetness
Instructions:
  1. Cook quinoa: Combine rinsed quinoa and broth/water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff and cool.
  2. Prepare vegetables: Dice bell pepper and red onion. Chop cilantro.
  3. Make the dressing: Whisk olive oil, lime juice, cumin, chili powder, salt, pepper, and optional sweetener.
  4. Assemble: In a large bowl, combine cooled quinoa, black beans, corn, bell pepper, red onion, and cilantro.
  5. Pour dressing over salad and toss.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Serve chilled or at room temperature.
Nutrition Tips: Complete plant-based protein, high in fiber, and packed with vitamins and minerals.

Creamy Avocado Pasta

A light yet satisfying, dairy-free pasta dish that's quick, wholesome, and flavorful. Ripe avocados create a luxurious, naturally creamy sauce, packed with healthy fats, fiber, and potassium. Fresh lemon juice adds tang, while garlic and basil provide aromatic depth. Use your favorite pasta. The sauce comes together while pasta cooks. Toss warm pasta with the fresh sauce immediately. Avoid reheating.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta of your choice (whole-wheat recommended)
  • 2 ripe avocados, pitted and scooped out
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, plus more for garnish
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2-4 tbsp reserved pasta water (as needed for consistency)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: cherry tomatoes, grilled chicken/shrimp, or chickpeas for added protein
  • Optional: nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor
Instructions:
  1. Cook pasta al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water, then drain.
  2. In a food processor or blender, combine avocado, garlic, basil, and lemon juice. Blend until smooth.
  3. Add reserved pasta water, 1 tbsp at a time, until desired consistency. Season.
  4. Add warm pasta to a large bowl. Pour sauce over and toss immediately.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished as desired.
Nutrition Tips: Packed with healthy monounsaturated fats from avocado, fiber, and fresh herbs.

Overnight Berry Chia Pudding

Effortless, healthy, and nutritious, perfect for busy mornings or a delightful snack. Chia seeds are powerhouses of fiber, Omega-3s, and plant-based protein. Use your favorite plant-based milk, natural sweetener, and vanilla. Mixed berries add antioxidants and sweetness. Simply combine ingredients, stir, and refrigerate overnight.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup plant-based milk (almond, oat, or coconut)
  • 1-2 tbsp maple syrup or agave nectar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen), plus more for topping
Instructions:
  1. In a jar, combine chia seeds, plant-based milk, sweetener, and vanilla extract. Stir well.
  2. Stir in the 1/2 cup of mixed berries.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Stir before serving. Add more milk if too thick.
  5. Top with additional fresh berries, granola, nuts, or seeds if desired.
  6. Enjoy chilled.
Nutrition Tips: Excellent source of fiber, Omega-3 fatty acids, and plant-based protein. Berries add antioxidants and vitamins.

Effortless Sheet Pan Chicken & Veggies

Simplify dinner with this effortless sheet pan meal. Minimal cleanup, maximum deliciousness. Lean chicken provides high-quality protein. Colorful vegetables like broccoli, bell peppers, red onion, and zucchini are packed with vitamins and antioxidants. Tossed with olive oil and a savory herb blend, the ingredients roast to perfection. Great for meal prepping!

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 small red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 medium zucchini, cut into half-moons (optional)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: a squeeze of lemon juice after roasting
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a large baking sheet.
  2. In a large bowl, combine chicken, broccoli, bell pepper, red onion, and zucchini (if using).
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat.
  4. Spread in a single layer on the baking sheet.
  5. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway, until chicken is cooked and vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Remove from oven. Squeeze lemon juice if desired.
  7. Serve hot.
Nutrition Tips: Balanced meal with lean protein and nutrient-dense vegetables. High in vitamins and fiber.

Warm Apple Cinnamon Baked Oats

Transform your breakfast with comforting Apple Cinnamon Baked Oats. Hearty oats provide sustained energy and fiber. Sweet-tart apples and cozy cinnamon create a delightful flavor. Oats are rich in beta-glucan for heart health. Fresh diced apples soften beautifully, and cinnamon adds warmth. Use plant-based milk, natural sweetener, and vanilla. Perfect for meal prepping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats (old-fashioned)
  • 1 large apple, peeled, cored, and diced into small pieces
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon, plus more for sprinkling
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup plant-based milk (almond, oat, or soy)
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup or honey (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: 1 tbsp chia seeds or ground flax seeds
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped walnuts or pecans
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a small baking dish.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine oats, diced apple, cinnamon, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk plant-based milk, maple syrup/honey, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir. Add optional seeds/nuts.
  5. Pour into baking dish and spread evenly.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes until set and golden.
  7. Remove from oven and cool slightly.
  8. Serve warm, optionally topped as desired.
Nutrition Tips: High in fiber for digestive health and sustained energy. Apples provide vitamins and antioxidants.

Zesty Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

Bring vibrant Mediterranean flavors to your table with this refreshing and satisfying salad. Bursting with fresh vegetables, hearty chickpeas, and a bright lemon-herb dressing. Chickpeas provide plant-based protein, fiber, and essential minerals. A colorful array of cucumber, cherry tomatoes, bell peppers, red onion, and Kalamata olives add nutrients. Easy to prepare and great for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 large cucumber, diced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped (optional, but highly recommended)
For the Lemon Vinaigrette:
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, bell pepper, red onion, olives, parsley, and mint (if using).
  2. In a small bowl, whisk olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour dressing over salad and toss.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Serve chilled or at room temperature.
Nutrition Tips: High in plant-based protein and fiber from chickpeas. Packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

Hearty Sweet Potato & Kale Hash

Elevate your breakfast or brunch with this vibrant and flavorful hash. A wholesome alternative packed with nutrient-dense vegetables. Sweet potatoes provide complex carbs, vitamins, and fiber. Kale adds powerful vitamins and antioxidants. Aromatic onions, garlic, and smoked paprika enhance flavor. Top with an egg for a complete meal, or add black beans/tofu for plant-based. Easy to prepare, adaptable with other vegetables or spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups kale, stems removed and chopped
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2-4 eggs (fried or poached) for topping
  • Optional: fresh parsley or chives for garnish
Instructions:
  1. Heat olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add diced sweet potatoes. Cook for 8-12 minutes until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. Add diced onion and cook for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and smoked paprika. Cook for 1 minute.
  5. Add chopped kale. Cook for 3-5 minutes until wilted.
  6. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. If adding eggs, prepare them while hash cooks.
  8. Serve hot, topped with eggs if desired.
Nutrition Tips: Rich in complex carbohydrates for energy, high in vitamins A, C, and K from sweet potatoes and kale, and packed with fiber.
